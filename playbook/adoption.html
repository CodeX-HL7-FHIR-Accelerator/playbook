Adoption & Value

Adoption in the real world is best driven by understanding at the Planning track what factors will move the market – including user demands, Government mandates, quality standards, and hopefully the promise of improved care and research during testing. It’s critical to Measure such improvements in the real world, to see where changes could make solutions even better (quality, cost, time) and help other participants engage.

Leverage community and outcomes to motivate widespread demand by users, integration into systems and processes

Health software vendors and related support companies will be motivated to invest in updates that enable new aspects of the Use Case when...

  • the pilots demonstrate potential value (increased benefit, decreased cost ...)
  • customers (providers, payers, researchers, etc.) demand such updates.
  • Government regulations and rules require such updates
  • in some cases, Payers or other stakeholders requires such updates
  • the vendors see a competitive advantage
  • someday, patients and caregivers will demand service levels that require interoperability

The steps documented leading to this point all should be aligned to maximize the possibility that all of the above are true.

Measure improvements to patient care and research across a growing community of stakeholders

From the start of adoption of the new standard and of the systems and workflows it powers, it will be important to assess the costs and benefits through the new workflow. Such assessments will help improve the standards, workflows, user interfaces, etc., and (when positive), help accelerate adoption and value.

Adoption in Production

Roles/Skills Needed

  • Use Case Lead, Technical Lead, Community Builder, Program Manager
  • Network to identify potential production participants
  • Evaluate potential production participants
    • What are their capabilities, experience, needs, and motivations
  • Establish Production Goals
  • Address Real World Data (RWD) use requirements
  • Track production system development, progress
    • Provide technical support as needed
  • Measure production impact
  • Evaluate results and determine next steps

Scale with Aligned Federal Initiatives

  • White House Cancer Moonshot supported by MITRE
  • OSTP and ARPA-H noted CodeX as an important partner in strengthening the nation’s clinical trial infrastructure
  • President’s Cancer Panel noted the importance of mCODE in their recommendations for NCI’s National Cancer Plan
  • CMS using mCODE in their Enhancing Oncology Model
  • FDA championing CodeX REMS Integration Use Case
  • CDC’s use of mCODE for Central Cancer Registry Reporting IG
  • ONC’s US Core Data For Interoperability (USCDI) + Canceris aligned with mCODE
  • ONC included select mCODE data elements in their USCDI+ proposed Quality Data Element List

CodeX Real World Traction

Industry Implementing mCODE and Extensions

  • 13 Vendors Implementing
  • 45% U.S. patients covered by mCODE consistent vendor systems
  • 80% of North American treatment sites supported by vendors adopting mCODE

International Traction

  • 10 countries exploring (6) or actively adopting (4) CodeX standards

Adoption for Routine Care

  • Oncolinicas, large cancer network in Latin America, adopted mCODE
    • Able to aggregate patient outcomes at previously unprecedented scale that correlate to scientific literature outcomes https://ascopubs.org/doi/abs/10.1200/JCO.2022.40.16_suppl.e13022
  • Mayo with pilots in routine use mCODE

  • Germany: routine exchange of genomics FHIR-based data
